# 15-05-2026 Single Neuron Modelling Theory Day 5

This session connects the lessons learnt from the Speaker's session and introduces the foundational principles of **single neuron modelling**, focusing on why and how neurons are represented mathematically and computationally in neuroscience.

##  Key Topics Covered

* Purpose of neuron modeling in neuroscience
* Principles of computational representation of neurons
* Why simplified models are used in brain science
* Introduction to model types in computational neuroscience

---

##  Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, participants should be able to:

* Explain why neuron models are used in neuroscience
* Describe basic principles behind computational modeling
* Understand trade-offs between biological realism and simplicity
* Recognize different approaches to neuron modeling

---

##  Preparation Note

Participants are encouraged to:

* Reflect on previous LIF neuron sessions
* Focus on the *purpose* of modeling, not just equations
* Engage actively with conceptual questions

---

##  Reflection Questions

* Why do neuroscientists use simplified neuron models?
* What are the advantages of computational modeling?
* How does modeling help us understand brain function?
